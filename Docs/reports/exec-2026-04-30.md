# Drift Daily Briefing — April 30, 2026

## Headline
Food database crossed 2,910 entries with Japanese and South Indian cuisine expansions; AI parse accuracy improved with attached-unit and decimal multiplier fixes.

## What Shipped This Period
- **Japanese home cooking expanded** — 30 new entries (ramen, tonkatsu, gyoza, miso soup, onigiri, etc.) for a cuisine that was missing from the DB
- **South Indian foods added** — 30 entries covering idli, dosa variants, sambar, rasam, pongal, and regional staples
- **AI parsing fix: "of" connector** — "cup of rice" no longer confuses the unit extractor; logging accuracy improved for common phrasing patterns
- **Decimal Nx multiplier phrases** — AI now handles "1.5x serving" and "2.5 portions" correctly in food logging
- **ToolRanker profiles for supplement & food timing** — supplement_insight and food_timing_insight tools now rank correctly in the intent pipeline; 16 new eval cases added
- **Tap-to-callout on weight chart** — tapping a data point shows the exact value inline; addresses a common friction point for weight tracking review

## What's Working Well
- Food DB velocity is high — 60+ entries added this sprint across Japanese and South Indian cuisines, with West African in progress
- AI eval harness is catching regressions early; the "of" connector fix was surfaced by eval before it hit TestFlight
- DriftCore extraction is stable — pure logic tests run in ~4s, giving fast iteration on parser and eval work

## Risks & Blockers

- **LLM eval gate is 12 min** — pre-TestFlight gate adds friction for food DB-only changes that don't touch the AI pipeline; consider a fast-path gate for non-AI commits
